MERIDIAN FINANCIAL GROUP
Data Governance Framework
Policy Reference: MFG-TECH-DG-004
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Review Cycle: Annual

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This framework defines Meridian Financial Group's (MFG) standards for data classification, access control, AI model governance, and data lifecycle management. It applies to all data assets — structured and unstructured — processed, stored, or transmitted by MFG systems or third-party vendors on MFG's behalf. This framework aligns with SEC Regulation S-P, GDPR (for EU-person data), CCPA, and the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0).

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION TIERS

All MFG data assets are assigned one of four classification tiers based on sensitivity and regulatory exposure:

TIER 1 — PUBLIC
Definition: Information approved for unrestricted public distribution (press releases, published research, marketing materials).
Controls: No access restrictions. Standard change management applies.

TIER 2 — INTERNAL
Definition: General business information not intended for external distribution but not sensitive if disclosed (internal memos, non-sensitive project documentation, general operational data).
Controls: Access limited to MFG employees and authorized contractors. Must not be stored on personal devices or transmitted via personal email.

TIER 3 — CONFIDENTIAL
Definition: Customer PII, account data, transaction records, non-public financial information, employee records, and pre-publication research.
Controls: Access on a need-to-know basis. Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) required. Must not be transmitted outside approved secure channels. Access logs retained for 3 years.

TIER 4 — RESTRICTED
Definition: Material non-public information (MNPI), merger and acquisition deal data, regulatory investigation files, and cryptographic key material.
Controls: Access limited to named individuals with documented business justification and senior management approval. Multi-factor authentication required. All access events logged in real time and reviewed weekly by the Information Security team. No cloud storage permitted unless using MFG-approved encrypted vaults.

3. ACCESS CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

Access to MFG data must comply with the following principles:

- Least Privilege: Users are granted the minimum level of access required for their role. Access rights are reviewed quarterly by system owners and adjusted as roles change.
- Segregation of Duties: Users with the ability to approve transactions or model deployments may not simultaneously hold administrator rights to the systems processing those transactions or deployments.
- Access Provisioning: All access requests must be submitted via the IT Service Portal and approved by the data owner and the employee's direct manager. Emergency access may be provisioned by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and must be reviewed and revoked within 24 hours.
- Offboarding: All system access must be revoked within 4 hours of employee termination notification.

4. AI MODEL GOVERNANCE AND APPROVAL PROCESS

All AI and machine learning models used in production at MFG must complete the following approval process before deployment:

Stage 1 — Model Registration: The model owner submits a Model Risk Assessment to the Model Risk Management (MRM) team. The assessment must include: model purpose and methodology, training data sources and data lineage documentation, known limitations and failure modes, and intended business use case.

Stage 2 — Independent Validation: The MRM team conducts an independent validation of the model within 30 business days of submission. Validation includes: backtesting on out-of-sample data, sensitivity analysis, and review of fairness and bias metrics for models impacting customers.

Stage 3 — Risk Rating: The MRM team assigns a risk rating (Low, Medium, High) based on the model's complexity, materiality of decisions it informs, and regulatory exposure. High-rated models require Chief Risk Officer (CRO) sign-off before deployment.

Stage 4 — Deployment Approval: Following successful validation, the model owner submits a Deployment Request to the Technology Change Control Board (CCB). CCB reviews deployment infrastructure, monitoring plan, and rollback procedures. CCB approval is required for all production deployments.

Stage 5 — Ongoing Monitoring: Deployed models must be monitored against defined performance thresholds. Any degradation exceeding 15% from baseline metrics triggers an automatic review by the MRM team. Annual full revalidation is required for all High-rated models; biennial for Medium; triennial for Low.

Generative AI models (including large language models) are automatically classified as High risk and require CRO sign-off, a dedicated data privacy impact assessment, and a human-in-the-loop review requirement for any customer-facing outputs.

5. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION POLICIES

Data must be retained for the following minimum periods, after which it must be securely deleted or anonymized:

- Customer account records: 7 years from account closure.
- Transaction records: 7 years from transaction date (SEC Rule 17a-4).
- Employee records: 7 years from date of termination.
- Model training data and validation reports: 5 years from model decommission date.
- Audit logs and access records: 3 years.
- Marketing and communications records: 3 years.

Secure deletion must use NIST SP 800-88 compliant methods. Physical media must be destroyed by a certified vendor with chain-of-custody documentation. A data deletion certificate must be retained for each deletion event.
