MERIDIAN FINANCIAL GROUP
Know Your Customer (KYC) Onboarding Guidelines
Policy Reference: MFG-COMP-KYC-002
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Review Cycle: Annual

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

These guidelines govern the identity verification and risk tiering procedures applied to all customers onboarded by Meridian Financial Group (MFG). They apply to retail banking, wealth management, corporate banking, and capital markets business lines. All onboarding must comply with FinCEN's Customer Due Diligence Rule (31 CFR § 1010.230) and FFIEC BSA/AML examination guidelines.

2. IDENTITY VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

All individual customers must provide the following at onboarding:

- Full legal name as it appears on a government-issued document.
- Date of birth.
- Current residential address (P.O. boxes are not acceptable as sole address).
- Government-issued photo identification — acceptable documents include: unexpired passport, national identity card, driver's license, or military ID. Foreign nationals must provide a passport. Expired documents are not accepted.

For legal entity customers (corporations, partnerships, LLCs, trusts):

- Legal entity name and jurisdiction of formation.
- Principal place of business address.
- Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) or equivalent foreign tax ID.
- Beneficial ownership certification identifying all individuals owning 25% or more of the entity and one individual with significant managerial control.
- Constitutional documents (articles of incorporation, partnership agreement, or trust deed).

Identity verification must be completed before account activation. Documentary verification is required; non-documentary verification methods (e.g., credit bureau checks) may supplement but not replace documentary verification for new accounts.

3. RISK TIERING

MFG uses a three-tier risk framework to determine the level of due diligence applied to each customer:

STANDARD DUE DILIGENCE (SDD) — Tier 1
Applies to: Retail customers with straightforward financial profiles, low-risk jurisdictions, standard consumer products.
Requirements: Basic identity verification as described in Section 2. Annual review via automated monitoring. No additional documentation required at onboarding.

ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD) — Tier 2
Applies to: Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), high-net-worth individuals with complex wealth structures, customers in FATF-listed jurisdictions, cash-intensive businesses, money service businesses (MSBs), and customers flagged by TMS for elevated activity.
Requirements: All SDD documentation plus:
- Source of funds declaration with supporting documentation (bank statements, tax returns, or audited financials for the prior two years).
- Source of wealth narrative (for PEPs and HNW clients).
- Reference letter from a regulated financial institution.
- Senior compliance officer sign-off on account approval.
- Semi-annual review cycle.
- In-person or video-verified identity confirmation.

SIMPLIFIED DUE DILIGENCE (SDD-S) — Tier 3
Applies to: Low-risk institutional counterparties that are themselves regulated financial institutions in FATF-compliant jurisdictions, government entities, and listed companies on recognized exchanges.
Requirements: Verification of regulated status and public identification only. No individual beneficial ownership documentation required. Annual review via automated monitoring.

4. ONGOING MONITORING OBLIGATIONS

All customers remain subject to ongoing monitoring throughout the account lifecycle:

- Automated transaction monitoring as defined in the AML Policy (MFG-COMP-AML-001).
- Annual risk re-assessment for all Tier 1 customers, semi-annual for Tier 2.
- Event-driven reviews triggered by: significant change in transaction volume or pattern, negative news screening alerts, PEP status change, or customer-initiated change in product usage.
- Negative news screening via third-party vendor must be run at onboarding and quarterly thereafter for Tier 2 customers.

5. DOCUMENT REFRESH CYCLES

KYC documentation must be refreshed at the following intervals:

- Tier 1 customers: Every 3 years, or sooner if a material change in customer profile is identified.
- Tier 2 (EDD) customers: Every 1 year. Source of funds documentation must be refreshed at each annual review.
- Tier 3 (SDD-S) customers: Every 5 years, or upon change in regulatory status.
- All customers: Immediately upon expiry of identity documents. Customers who fail to provide updated documentation within 30 days of expiry notice will have account activity restricted pending resolution.

6. ESCALATION AND EXCEPTIONS

Requests for onboarding exceptions (e.g., reduced documentation requirements) must be submitted in writing to the Chief Compliance Officer and documented in the customer file. No account may be opened on an exception basis without written CCO approval.
