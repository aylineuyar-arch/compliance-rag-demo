MERIDIAN FINANCIAL GROUP
Trade Surveillance and Personal Account Dealing Policy
Policy Reference: MFG-COMP-TRD-003
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Review Cycle: Annual

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes Meridian Financial Group's (MFG) framework for trade surveillance, personal account dealing controls, and restricted list administration. It applies to all employees, contractors, and agents who execute, facilitate, or have access to information regarding client or proprietary trading activity. MFG is committed to preventing market abuse, insider trading, front-running, and conflicts of interest in accordance with SEC Rule 10b-5, FINRA Rules 3110 and 4511, and applicable MiFID II obligations for EU-facing business.

2. PRE-TRADE MONITORING

Pre-trade controls are designed to prevent prohibited transactions before they occur:

- All employee orders in securities on the Restricted List (see Section 4) are blocked at order entry by the trading system. Employees attempting to enter such orders receive an automated denial with instructions to contact the Compliance desk.
- Personal account dealing orders exceeding $25,000 in a single security require pre-clearance via the employee compliance portal. Pre-clearance requests must be submitted at least 1 business day in advance and are valid for 2 business days from approval.
- Front-office employees in possession of material non-public information (MNPI) are subject to an information barrier (Chinese Wall). Trades in the affected security by information barrier-designated employees are blocked system-wide until the barrier is lifted by the Chief Compliance Officer.
- All algorithmic and systematic strategies must pass a pre-deployment review by the Trading Compliance team before going live. Reviews assess for potential layering, spoofing, or quote stuffing patterns.

3. POST-TRADE MONITORING

Post-trade surveillance is conducted daily by the Trade Surveillance team using automated monitoring tools:

- Pattern-of-trading alerts are generated for: wash trades, marking-the-close activity, excessive cancellation ratios (greater than 80% of orders), and front-running patterns (employee personal account trades in the same direction as client orders within a 5-business-day window).
- All alerts are assigned a severity score (Low, Medium, High, Critical) by the surveillance system. Medium and above alerts require analyst review within 2 business days of generation.
- Cross-market surveillance reviews are conducted monthly to identify coordinated activity across asset classes or accounts.
- Trade reconstruction reports are retained for 7 years in accordance with SEC Rule 17a-4 requirements.

4. RESTRICTED LIST PROCEDURES

The Restricted List contains securities in which MFG has a current advisory, underwriting, or other relationship that creates a conflict of interest or MNPI exposure:

- The Restricted List is maintained by the Compliance department and updated in real time as new engagements are initiated or concluded.
- Securities are added to the Restricted List upon engagement letter signing and removed within 2 business days of transaction close or engagement termination.
- Employees are not notified of specific additions to the Restricted List; the trading system enforces restrictions automatically.
- The Watch List (a non-public subset) flags securities under active monitoring where trading is not yet restricted but is subject to enhanced post-trade review.
- Exceptions to Restricted List prohibitions (e.g., for hedging existing positions) require written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer and General Counsel.

5. PERSONAL ACCOUNT DEALING RULES

All employees are subject to the following personal account dealing requirements:

- Employees must disclose all personal brokerage accounts to Compliance within 10 days of hire and within 30 days of opening a new account thereafter.
- Duplicate account statements must be submitted quarterly for all personal brokerage accounts held at institutions other than MFG.
- A minimum holding period of 30 calendar days applies to all personal account securities transactions. Short-term trading (buy and sell within 30 days in the same security) is prohibited absent a documented exception.
- Short selling of MFG's own securities by employees is prohibited at all times.

6. ESCALATION PROCEDURES FOR ALERTS

When a trade surveillance alert is escalated for review, the following procedure applies:

- Level 1 (Analyst) Review: The assigned analyst must review the alert, gather relevant context (order history, communications, news events), and document findings within 2 business days. If the analyst determines the activity is benign, they document the rationale and close the alert.
- Level 2 (Senior Compliance Officer) Escalation: If the Level 1 analyst cannot resolve the alert or identifies potentially suspicious activity, the alert is escalated to a Senior Compliance Officer within 1 business day. The Senior Compliance Officer has 5 business days to conduct an enhanced review.
- Level 3 (Chief Compliance Officer / Legal) Escalation: If the Senior Compliance Officer identifies potential market abuse or regulatory violations, the matter is immediately escalated to the Chief Compliance Officer and General Counsel. A regulatory referral to FINRA or the SEC may be initiated within 10 business days.
- All escalation decisions, rationale, and outcomes must be documented in the case management system and retained for 7 years.
