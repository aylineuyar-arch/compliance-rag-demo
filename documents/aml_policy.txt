MERIDIAN FINANCIAL GROUP
Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy
Policy Reference: MFG-COMP-AML-001
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Review Cycle: Annual

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes Meridian Financial Group's (MFG) framework for detecting, preventing, and reporting money laundering activities in accordance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the USA PATRIOT Act, and applicable FinCEN guidance. This policy applies to all business lines, employees, and third-party agents operating on behalf of MFG.

2. TRANSACTION MONITORING THRESHOLDS

MFG operates an automated transaction monitoring system (TMS) calibrated to detect unusual or suspicious activity. The following thresholds trigger mandatory review:

- Cash transactions of $10,000 or more in a single business day require Currency Transaction Report (CTR) filing with FinCEN within 15 calendar days.
- Structured transactions (multiple transactions designed to evade the $10,000 threshold) are flagged when cumulative daily cash activity exceeds $9,000 across two or more transactions from the same customer or related accounts.
- Wire transfers of $3,000 or more require full originator and beneficiary recordkeeping under the Travel Rule.
- International wire transfers of $10,000 or more to or from jurisdictions on the FATF high-risk list require enhanced documentation and senior compliance officer sign-off prior to execution.
- Unusual account activity relative to customer baseline — defined as transactions exceeding 300% of the customer's 90-day average — are escalated for Level 1 review within 48 hours.

3. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING (SAR)

When a transaction or pattern of activity is identified as potentially suspicious, the following procedures apply:

- Level 1 analysts must complete the initial review within 5 business days of alert generation.
- If escalated to Level 2, the reviewing compliance officer has 10 business days to complete the investigation.
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) must be filed with FinCEN within 30 calendar days of the date suspicious activity is initially identified. A 60-day extension is available with documented justification.
- SAR filings are strictly confidential. Disclosure of a SAR filing or the existence of an investigation to the subject of the report (tipping off) is prohibited under 31 U.S.C. § 5318(g)(2).
- SARs must be retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of filing.

4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

All new customers must complete CDD procedures before account opening:

- Collect and verify full legal name, date of birth, physical address, and government-issued identification (passport, national ID, or driver's license).
- Obtain beneficial ownership information for legal entity customers — any individual owning 25% or more of the entity must be identified and verified.
- Assess the customer's expected transaction activity, source of funds, and business purpose.
- Assign a risk rating (Low, Medium, High) based on customer type, geography, industry, and product usage.
- High-risk customers require Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) as defined in the KYC Guidelines (MFG-COMP-KYC-002).

5. RECORD RETENTION REQUIREMENTS

MFG must retain the following records for a minimum of 5 years:

- All CDD and EDD documentation collected at onboarding and during periodic review.
- CTR and SAR filings and all supporting investigation materials.
- Transaction records for wire transfers subject to the Travel Rule.
- TMS alert records, including disposition notes, analyst decisions, and escalation history.
- Training records demonstrating employee completion of annual AML training.

Records must be stored in a tamper-evident format and must be retrievable and producible to regulators within 3 business days upon request.

6. TRAINING AND ACCOUNTABILITY

All employees with customer-facing or transaction-processing responsibilities must complete annual AML training. Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may expose MFG and individual employees to civil or criminal penalties.
